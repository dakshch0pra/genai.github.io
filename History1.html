1950

Turing Test

Alan Turing Alan Turing proposed the Turing Test, laying the foundational concept for machines mimicking human intelligence, which is a core aspect of generative AI.

1952

Introduction of Machine Learning

Arthur Samuel developed the first machine learning algorithm for playing checkers and introduces the term machine learning

1956

Term Artificial Intelligence was coined at the Dartmouth Conference

1957

The Perceptron

Frank Rosenblatt created the Perceptron, the first neural network capable of being trained, though it had limitations due to its single-layer design.

1961

ELIZA

Joseph Weizenbaum developed ELIZA, an early chatbotthat uses natural language processing to simulate conversation.

1972

Facial Recognition

Ann B. Lesk, Leon D. Harmon, and A. J. Goldstein significantly improved facial recognition accuracy by developing specific markers for automatic identification.

1973

First AI Winter(1973-1979)

Funding reduced due to unfulfilled promises.
Continued for 6 years.

1975

The Cognitron

Kunihiko Fukushima developed the Cognitron, the first functional multilayered artificial neural network.

1979

The NeoCognitron

Kunihiko Fukushima developed the Cognitron, the first Deep Learning neural network capable of hierarchical pattern recognition.

1982

The Hopfield Net

John Hopfield introduced the Hopfield net, a neural network that mimics human memory retrieval processes.

1984

Second AI Winter(1984-1990)

Funding reduced due to unfulfilled promises.
Continued for 6 years.

1986

Backpropagation Algorithm

It was first introduced in the 1960s and in 1986 improved popularized by David Rumelhart and his team in the famous 1986 paper.

1989

ZIP Code recognition

Yann LeCun and his team used backpropagation with neural networks making a benchmark in Deep Learning

1990

Paper on "Boosting"

Robert Schapire's paper on "boosting" explained conersion of weak learners to strong leaders using machine learning algorithms.

1997

Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) Networks

Juergen Schmidhuber and Sepp Hochreiter introduced LSTM networks, crucial for sequence prediction and speech recognition.

1999

Nvidia's GeForce 256 GPU

This enhanced computational speeds, benefiting AI and machine learning applications.

2004

The Face Recognition Grand Challenge(2004-2006)

Funded by the U.S. government, leads to major advancements in facial recognition technology.

2006

Geoffrey Hinton coins the term deep learning

2011

Siri, the first functional digital virtual assistant, is introduced with the iPhone 4S.

2012

AlexNet wins ImageNet Challenge

AlexNet, a deep neural network, wins the ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge, showcasing the power of deep learning.

2014

Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)

Ian Goodfellow and colleagues introduce GANs, a framework enabling machines to generate highly realistic images.

2016

Development of WaveNet

Google's DeepMind develops WaveNet, a deep generative model of raw audio waveforms capable of generating natural-sounding speech and music.

2017

Development of processive GANs

NVIDIA develops Progressive GANs, which produce high-resolution, photo-realistic images by progressively adding layers during training.

2018

OpenAI releases GPT 2

OpenAI's GPT-2, a generative language model, demonstrates AI's ability to write coherent and contextually relevant text.

2019

OpenAI introduces DALL-E

2020

OpenAI launches GPT-3

2022

OpenAI releases ChatGPT

2023

OpenAI introduces GPT-4
